David Goggins

About Me

David Goggins is an American ultramarathon runner, ultra-distance cyclist, triathlete, motivational speaker, and author, renowned for his mental toughness and endurance. Born on February 17, 1975, he overcame a challenging childhood marked by abuse, poverty, and discrimination. Struggling with obesity and low self-esteem, Goggins transformed his life by losing over 100 pounds in three months to join the U.S. Navy SEALs, an extraordinary testament to his willpower.

In the military, Goggins distinguished himself by completing Hell Week three times and serving as an Air Force Tactical Air Control Party member. His military experiences forged his mental and physical endurance, which he later applied to endurance sports. He has excelled in ultramarathons, including the notoriously difficult Badwater 135, ultra-distance cycling, and triathlons. Goggins' philosophy is centered on the "40% rule," which suggests that when one thinks they are done, they are only 40% done. This mindset is detailed in his memoir, "Can't Hurt Me: Master Your Mind and Defy the Odds," which has inspired millions to overcome their obstacles and strive for greatness. As a motivational speaker, Goggins encourages audiences to embrace discomfort, push past limits, and cultivate resilience. David Goggins exemplifies perseverance and mental fortitude, transforming from a troubled youth into a symbol of endurance and inspiration. His story demonstrates the power of determination and hard work, inspiring countless individuals to pursue their potential against all odds.
